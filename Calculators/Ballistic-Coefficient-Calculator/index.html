Ballistic Coefficient Calculator

Mass affects the projectile's inertia. Higher mass typically increases the ballistic coefficient.
Diameter is used to calculate the cross-sectional area, influencing air resistance.
The drag coefficient quantifies how streamlined the projectile is. Lower Cd reduces air resistance.
Initial velocity determines the speed of the projectile at launch, affecting its range and trajectory.
Wind speed influences the drift of the projectile during flight.
The launch angle determines the trajectory and maximum range of the projectile.
